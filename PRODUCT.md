# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Four distinct audiences, often overlapping:

- **AI project builders**: Teams launching tokens who want fair, transparent distribution without VC gatekeeping. Context: researching launchpad options, comparing fairness mechanisms, high skepticism of opaque processes.
- **Crypto investors and degens**: Early-access hunters seeking high-potential AI projects. Context: fast decision-making, scanning multiple opportunities, price-sensitive, liquidity-aware.
- **Content creators and influencers**: Analysts, thread-writers, meme-makers who want to monetize research and community contributions via $INFO (yap-to-earn). Context: already active on Twitter/Telegram, motivated by status and earning.
- **Traders and liquidity providers**: Participants in auctions and post-launch DEX pools. Context: watching price mechanics, assessing liquidity depth, post-auction market activity.

## Product Purpose

Info.Launch is a decentralized launchpad for AI-native projects combining Dutch auction token distribution with InfoFi, a mechanism that tokenizes community attention and rewards high-signal content with $INFO tokens. The tagline "Say It. Earn It. Spend It." captures the loop: contribute content, earn tokens, use tokens to participate in launches.

Success looks like: projects launching with genuine community buy-in, content creators rewarded for real signal (not hype), and investors accessing opportunities that were previously gated by VC relationships.

## Brand Personality

Bold, transparent, community-native.

Voice: Direct and slightly irreverent. Calls out the problem plainly ("No more VC gatekeeping"). Comfortable with crypto-native language (degens, yap-to-earn, alpha) without being exclusionary. Confident without being arrogant.

Emotional goal: Make users feel like insiders who finally have access, not outsiders looking in.

## Anti-references

- Neon-on-black memecoin / pump.fun aesthetic: chaotic, low-trust, disposable
- Dense DeFi protocol sites (Aave, Compound style): utilitarian, intimidating, designed for power users only
- Generic SaaS landing pages: "streamline your workflow", pastel gradients, stock-photo teams
- Presale scam aesthetics: countdown timers, inflated promises, dark patterns

## Design Principles

1. **Transparency as aesthetics**: The design should make the mechanism legible, not mystify it. Dutch auction mechanics, tokenomics, InfoFi rewards: show the system, don't hide it behind vague marketing language.
2. **Community-earned credibility**: Nothing feels handed down from above. Hierarchy is flat; users and builders are on the same level. The visual language reflects participation, not gatekeeping.
3. **Confident without hype**: Bold moves in color, type, and composition, but no exaggeration. The product speaks for itself.
4. **Accessible signal**: AI dashboards and real data are central to the value prop. Design should make information readable to motivated newcomers, not just crypto natives.
5. **Motion with purpose**: Animations are part of the energy but never obscure the message. Every motion has a reason.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Color system uses four distinct brand hues across the spectrum; ensure no meaning is conveyed by color alone. Reduced motion preference respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Touch targets 44x44px minimum on mobile. Minimum 16px body text on mobile viewports.
